Privacy & Terms of Use

This course includes content for the Mobile CSP Curriculum and an option to interact with other learners in an online discussion forum. All coursework should be completed by August 22, 2014.

Successful completion of required assessments will be recorded, and records will be maintained by the College for 180 days after the conclusion of the workshop.

Participants are expected to comply with the academic honesty policies of The College of St. Scholastica and Trinity College. It is expected that all assessments are to be completed by each participant independently except for collaborative work. The College policies relating to academic honesty, as specified in the Student Handbook, will be followed.

Academic honesty and integrity are highly valued in our online community. Academic honesty directly concerns ethical behaviors which affect both the academic environment and the civic community. Academic dishonestly seriously violates the integrity of the academic enterprise and will not be tolerated at St. Scholastica. The full text of the CSS Academic Honesty Policy is found in the Student Handbook online.

Participants are also expected to comply with standards of our community and to demonstrate Internet etiquette in their interactions with others and their use of online resources. Failure to comply with these standards may result in removal from the course.

Analytics data regarding participant interaction with course materials will be collected and analyzed in the aggregate.

I understand that neither The College of St. Scholastica nor Trinity College will sell or share my contact information with outside parties, but may use it to provide information about other courses and degree programs.
